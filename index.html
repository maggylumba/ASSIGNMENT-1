AFRICAN DISHES

Africa is well known across boarders for its nature, the beauty of wild animals like the Giraffe, Lion, Zebra,
which are a source of tourist attraction. The rich cultural diversity and conservative nature of its citizens despite
western influence remains a mystery to many.
Even after colonisation period, many Africans still uphold the ways of their forefathers, from rites of passage to naming ceremonies.
However, one cannot mention Africa without talking about the nice traditional dishes that come with it.
The unforgettable taste made with love and kindness, from our mama's pot and the sacred recipes passed down from one generation to another.
In this article, we feature some of the best African dishes in the African continent.

UGALI

Ugali is a kenyan delicacy introduced to the Kenyans in the coastal region by the portuguese. But for Kenyans, it is mostly consumed in the western part of Kenya.
cook Ugali, you need to dry Maize popularly known as corn in the USA.
Maize is then taken to a grinding machine which turns it into flour. A pot with water is put on a fire and brought to a
boiling point after which the flour is poured in and stired into a hard round paste. Its served with traditiona stew like chisaka while hot.

JOLLOF RICE

Jollof rice is a Nigerian dish made from rice and ground beef.
The recipe is also cooked in Senegal and Ghana.
There are five types of jollof rice;

    Suya jollof rice
    Jambalaya rice
    Oven baked jollof rice
    Sea food jollof rice
    Basmati jollof rice

KUKU PAKA

Kuku paka is a Tanzanian dish cooked in coconut sauce after being grilled to get a smoky taste.
Its also called kuku wa nazi.
It is served with hot chapati ugali or Matoke.

LUWOMBO CHICKEN

Luwombo chicken is a Ugandan dish where different kinds
of ingredients are stuffed together with chicken and cooked in banana leaves.
The covering helps retain the nutrients and also creates a nice taste.
Because cooking oil is not used to cook it, many people like it for health reasons.
It is served with Ugali, Matoke or Chapati.

MATOOKE

Matooke is a Ugandan staple food cooked from green bananas.
To cook bananas, you need to peel them and wash then put water in a pot to boil.
The water is then drained and the banana is smashed to create a smooth paste,
which is then wrapped in banana leaves to keep it hot.
It can then be served with chicken stew or beef stew.

MUKIMO

Mukimo is a kenyan dish made by the people in the mountain the Agikuyu.
It is made from irish potatoes and pumpkin leaves.
The two are boiled and mashed together to get a fine paste.
Other people add boiled corn which makes it more healthier.
Mukimo can be served with beef stew and cabbage.

MUTHOKOI

Muthokoi is a kamba delicacy .
It is prepared by removing husks on maize and boiling it with peas or beans.

GITHERI

Githeri is a mixture of maize and beans boiled together and fried for taste.
Some people can however eat without frying. Githeri is very
nutritious being a combination of carbohydrates and proteins.

It will therefore be the best decision visiting Africa and enjoying some of this dishes.
